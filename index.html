Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable

Once upon a time, there was a city named Swanport, known for its towering, unsecured buildings. They were warned not to build them so high without proper risk assessment, but they didn't listen.

One day, out of nowhere, an earthquake struck. The citizens were terrified, they had never experienced such a disaster before and watched in horror as all their buildings crumbled to the ground.

The citizens banded together and shared whatever resources they had left. However, they knew it wouldn't be enough to last them long. With no clear plan, they felt hopeless and wondered how they would survive.

Days passed until the Red Cross arrived, providing much-needed resources, food, and temporary shelter.

With their help, the people of Swanport began to rebuild their homes and city, placing a strong emphasis on disaster risk reduction. They implemented earthquake-resistant building designs to ensure their city would be safe and resilient in the future. The people of Swanport also prioritized sustainability, making sure their city was inclusive, safe, and sustainable for all.

Through this experience, the citizens of Swanport learned a valuable lesson about the importance of disaster risk reduction and sustainability. They hoped that their story would inspire others to work together towards creating a safer and more sustainable future for everyone.
